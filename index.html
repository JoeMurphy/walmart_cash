Rollbacks – Category Strategy

Rollbacks

Rollbacks function as a traditional price-cut lever, heavily utilized to drive velocity in discretionary and durable goods categories.

  • Dominant Categories: Toys, Furniture, and Tires lead the pack, indicating a strategy focused on big-ticket and seasonal items.
  • Strategic Intent: The depth of discount (median 23%) suggests these are designed to clear inventory or compete on price-sensitive hardlines.
  • Secondary Mix: Presence in Vitamins and Hair Care shows selective use in consumables to drive unit volume.

Walmart Cash – Loyalty & Frequency

Walmart Cash

Walmart Cash operates as a trial and product discovery mechanism, primarily deployed in high-frequency consumable categories.

  • Core Focus: Heavily concentrated in Health & Beauty (Hair Care, Skin Care) and Grocery (Snacks), targeting the weekly basket.
  • Value Proposition: With a median reward of 22%, it offers significant value to repeat shoppers without lowering shelf price.
  • Shopper Behavior: Encourages brand stickiness and basket building in competitive CPG categories.

Walmart Cash – CPG Partnerships

Walmart Cash

Walmart Cash is defined by deep partnerships with major CPG conglomerates, functioning as a digital trade spend vehicle.

  • Power Players: Procter & Gamble is the dominant player, leveraging the platform for its portfolio of household brands.
  • Key Partners: Unilever, Johnson & Johnson, and General Mills are also heavy users, aligning with their focus on customer lifetime value.
  • Strategic Alignment: Suggests a coordinated effort between Walmart and top CPGs to drive digital engagement.
